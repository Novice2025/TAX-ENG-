Understanding financial and economic terms.

This lesson focuses on distinguishing between common terms related to costs, taxes, and payments.

Tax (Noun)

A **tax** is a mandatory financial charge or other levy imposed on an individual or a legal entity exclusively by a government entity (municipal, state, or federal).

**Example:** "The government announced a new sales **tax** on luxury goods."

Tax only for government.

Fee (Noun)

A **fee** is a payment made for a service or privilege, rather than a tax. It is usually paid to a professional or organization.

**Example:** "The bank charges a monthly **fee** for checking accounts."

Fee for payment transaction, professional services.

Rate (Noun)

A **rate** is a fixed price paid or charged for something, especially services, calculated in relation to a specific unit of time, quantity, or another measure.

**Example:** "The interest **rate** on the loan is 5%."

Rate - combined with a percentage sign.

Tariff (Noun)

A **tariff** is a tax or duty to be paid on a particular class of imports or exports. It is a specific type of tax, typically used in international trade.

**Example:** "The country imposed a 25% **tariff** on imported steel."

Tariff - is between / among countries and involved diplomacy - Geopolitics.

Duty (Noun)

A **duty** is a tax on specific items, most often on imported or exported goods. The term is very similar to `tariff` but can also refer to a tax on things like alcohol or tobacco.

**Example:** "Travelers must pay a **duty** on items purchased abroad."

Duty - is products going to or from another country - even the foreign company is based in Brazil - In portuguese we say encargos e taxas aduaneiras.

Fare (Noun)

A **fare** is the price charged for a person to travel on public transportation, such as a bus, a train, a taxi, or a plane.

**Example:** "The bus **fare** has increased to two dollars."

Fare - is only used for transport and taxi. (It is tarifa in Portuguese).

Charge (Noun & Verb)

A **charge** is a broad, general term for an amount of money that has to be paid, especially for a service or goods. It is more general than a `fee`. As a verb, to **charge** means to ask for a specific amount of money as a price for a service or goods.

**Example (noun):** "There's a delivery **charge** on this order."

**Example (verb):** "The mechanic will **charge** you for the repairs."

Charge - is for transactions - same as fee.

The Difference: Fee vs. Charge

The key difference is scope. A **fee** is a specific type of payment for a set service (e.g., an annual gym membership fee). A **charge** is a more general term for an amount owed, which can include various costs. While all fees are charges, not all charges are fees.

**Example:** "My phone bill had a monthly service **fee** and an extra **charge** for international calls."

Levy (Verb)

To **levy** means to impose a tax, fee, or fine. It is the verb used to describe the action of collecting a payment.

**Example:** "The city plans to **levy** a new tax to fund the construction project."

Levy - is the verb for tax - taxar.

Taxed (Verb)

**Taxed** is the past tense of the verb `to tax`. It means to have a tax imposed on something.

**Example:** "My income was **taxed** at a higher rate this year."

Taxed - is the verb for tax - taxar.

Waive (Verb) & Waiver (Noun)

To **waive** is a verb that means to choose not to use or require something, like a fee or a rule. A **waiver** is the official act or document that records the waiving of a right or claim.

**Example (verb):** "The airline agreed to **waive** the baggage fee."

**Example (noun):** "You will need to sign a **waiver** to participate in the event."

Waive - isentar.

Waiver - isento.

Bonus: Key Distinctions

Here's a quick summary to help you remember the differences between some of the most common terms.

Tax vs. Fee

**Tax:** a mandatory payment to the government.

**Fee:** a payment for a service or privilege, usually to a private company or professional.

Fine vs. Fee

**Fine:** a monetary penalty for breaking a rule or law.

**Fee:** a payment for a service received.

Interest vs. Rate

**Interest:** the cost of borrowing money.

**Rate:** the percentage used to calculate the interest.

Surcharge (Noun)

A **surcharge** is an extra fee or charge added to an existing price. It is often used to cover an extra cost, such as a fuel surcharge or a holiday surcharge.

**Example:** "There is a 3% **surcharge** for using a credit card."

Surcharge - a charge on top of a charge. (Sobretaxa).

Fine (Noun)

A **fine** is a sum of money imposed as a penalty for an offense or wrongdoing. It is a form of punishment, not a payment for a service.

**Example:** "He had to pay a **fine** for a parking violation."

Fine - a monetary penalty for a mistake. (Multa).

Reimbursement (Noun)

A **reimbursement** is the act of paying back money to a person who has already spent it.

**Example:** "You will get a **reimbursement** for your travel expenses."

Reimbursement - getting money back from our company. (Reembolso pago pela empresa por despesas pagas).

Invoice / Tax Invoice (Noun)

An **invoice** is a commercial document that itemizes a transaction between a buyer and a seller. If goods or services were purchased on credit, the invoice will specify the payment terms. A **tax invoice** is a type of invoice that meets specific government requirements for taxation, often required for businesses.

**Example:** "The company sent a monthly **invoice** for the consulting services. We need a **tax invoice** for this purchase to claim the VAT."

Invoice - a bill you send to a client. (Fatura)

Tax Invoice - a bill for tax purposes. (Nota Fiscal)

Interest (Noun)

**Interest** is a charge for borrowing money, typically expressed as an annual percentage of the loan amount. It is what a borrower pays a lender for the use of the money.

**Example:** "The bank offers a low-interest rate on home loans."

Interest - custo do dinheiro emprestado. (Juros).

Late Interest / Default Interest (Noun)

**Late interest** or **default interest** is an extra interest rate charged on a payment that is overdue. This is separate from the original interest rate and serves as a penalty for being late.

**Example:** "If you miss the payment deadline, you will be charged **late interest**."

Late Interest - juros cobrados por atraso no pagamento. (Mora / Juros de mora).

Late Fee / Penalty (Noun)

A **late fee** or **penalty** is a flat fee charged for failing to make a payment by the due date. Unlike late interest, which is calculated as a percentage, a late fee is a fixed amount.

**Example:** "A **late fee** of $25 will be added to your account if payment is not received on time."

Late Fee - uma taxa fixa cobrada por atraso no pagamento. (Multa).
